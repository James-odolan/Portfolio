Visual Communications Project

Leeds Museum Promotion

This was one of the early graphic design projects that I had during my time at Leeds Beckett University, as you can see there was a heavy amount of research for this project, into branding and visual imagery for the promotion. The task was to create an imagined rebranding for Leeds City Museum including brand guidelines, colour scheme, fonts and all other necessary matertials.

Poster Work

As you can see in these ancient Egypt poster designs this is where I really started to learn more about graphic design. Playing around with a visual effects like graininess, overlay styles and outer glows. This is still early on so some work was still yet to be desired but I think it’s a great example of the growth that I’ve experienced when contrasting this stuff with my new work. I took advantage of this project to experiment as much as possible.

Here again you can see the posters logo designs I was trying to work on colour theory so as you can see are experimented with different shades and contrasts and learnt more about colour libraries and what is best to apply in which context furthermore learning about conventions of colour theory.

Skills Used

I learned to use the tools in Adobe Illustrator to shape and edit certain aspects of my design such as the creation of the rebranded museum logo, further developing my skills in the software

In Adobe Photoshop I used the pen tool, gradients and other tools to create different designs for exhibition posters. This also taught me to work my way round issues with the variety of tools available.

Planning and preparation was a key takeaway. I heavily researched into other brands doing similar campaigns, implementing things such as brand guidelines, colour scheme and fonts.
